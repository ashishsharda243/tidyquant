Robotic UVC sanitizer to Fight COVID-19

At Tidyquant, we have been doing our research on Coronavirus. In doing so, we studied its biological structure, size, genetic material and genetic injection into human cell. Further, our research navigated us to investigate Ultraviolet spectrum and its germicidal properties. Ultraviolet (UV) radiation is sandwiched between X rays and visible light, i.e., between 100 and 400 nm. The UV spectrum is separated into Vacuum UV (100-200 nm), Far-UVC (200-222 nm), UVC (222-280 nm), UVB (280-315), and UVA (315-400 nm) as shown in the figure.

Since the size of microorganisms found in air, water and on surfaces typically varies from 50 nm to 2µm, the genetic material (DNA or RNA) of these microorganisms can be disrupted by irradiating UV light with short (comparable to the size of microorganism) wavelengths. By disrupting genetic material, the microorganism will be inactivated because DNA or RNA present in nucleic acid are responsible for microbial replication and protein synthesis in human cell.

In order to combat COVID-19 pandemic situation, we came up with a robotic sanitizer device that uses short wavelength UV radiation to kill bacteria and viruses. By deploying such devices in hospitals, public transports, schools, supermarkets, etc., our proposed solution can help government to prevent COVID-19’s spread across the country on a massive scale.

Conventional UV lamps operating at 254nm wavelengths are readily available and have been used for bacterial disinfection since last 2 decades. However, UV radiation at 254nm wavelength can penetrate outer layer of human skin, and therefore it cannot be exposed to humans directly.

On the other hand, a much shorter wavelength (222 nm) of Far-UVC radiation cannot penetrate outer layer of human skin and therefore it’s a suitable candidate for our proposed solution. This Robotic UVC sanitizer device will be equipped with microcontroller board, Arduino board, electromagnetic relays, Bluetooth module and PIR sensors. Once implemented, device can be operated remotely using an android mobile device over a Bluetooth channel. As a safety measure, PIR sensors will be employed in order to detect any human motion around proximity and to trigger an alarm for the operator. Industrial grade stepper motors would allow UVC robot to perform sanitization at a high degree of precision.

Portable air sanitizer uses long tubes of UVC lamps and a couple of propeller fans in order to condition the air present in the room. This can be very useful application for patient wards (in hospitals) where COVID-19 patients are being treated. Once powered up, it will keep sanitizing air in the surroundings. On the other hand, UVC drone sanitizer can be very useful in disinfecting surfaces that are not easily reachable.
