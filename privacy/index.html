Privacy & Policy















Here at Tidyquant we abide by all regulations and laws to ensure we are both a successful and ethical business, at no point would we look to exploit anyone business or citizen to succeed. Whilst we work on any of our contracts we sign non-disclosure agreements ensuring any data we use will be privileged and not used outside the content of our contract.

Introduction

Tidyquant Ltd respects the privacy of its users, clients and staff alike. We are committed to protecting the information that you share with us, whether it’s directly or through using our services. We want to be transparent in our efforts and practices regarding the data we may collect when you encounter any part of our organisation.

Data we collect from you

Your personal data will be collected when you apply for a job or register for any query. The personal data collected include information that you provide, such as your name, e-mail address, telephone number required to apply for a job. These types of data are not precluded by the new regulations.

Your devices information will also be automatically collected which you used to interact with our site. Some of the information that will be automatically collected may include your IP address, device identifier, web browser, and browsing information collected through cookies on our site. We may also automatically collect information about how you use the site, such as what you have searched for and viewed on the site. The information automatically collected will be associated with any personal data you have provided.

Types of data

We may collect two types of data from our Users:

  • Non-identifiable and anonymous information (often referred to as “Non-Personal Data”) this is data which we are not aware of the identity of the User from which we have collected the Non-Personal Data.
  • Individually identifiable information (referred to as “Personal Data”) where we may be able to identify an individual or the information may be of a private and/or sensitive nature.

Tidyquant will not request any “Sensitive Personal Data” including, information concerning an individual’s racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or similar beliefs, trade union membership (or non-membership), physical or mental health condition, criminal offences or related proceedings, or any other data considered as sensitive under applicable law unless it is relating to your employment by Tidyquant or an application for employment.

How we use the information we collect

  • We will require registration information to provide our services for your company.
  • To assess your data against vacancies which we judge may be suitable for you.
  • To enable you to apply online for jobs or to subscribe to alerts about jobs we think may be of interest to you.
  • To improve our customer service and to make our services more valuable to you.
  • To answer your questions and enquiries.
  • To send you details of reports, promotions, offers, networking and client events and general information about the industry sectors which we think might be of interest to you where you have consented to being contacted for such purpose.
  • To provide your services to our clients.
  • To maintain our business relationship with client or candidate.
  • To maintain, expand and develop our business.
  • We may also use first and third-party Cookies and Other Tracking Technologies to manage our Sites and our services and collect analytics about how you use them.

Legal basis for processing

The legal basis for collecting and using your data vary depending on the way in which you are interacting with our products and services. We collect and use your data only where:

  • We require it for the provision of the services, to protect the safety and security of the services, and without such data we would not be able to provide the services.
  • You have given consent for us to use it for specific purposes where you have provided consent.
  • We need to process your data to fulfil a legal obligation.
  • It satisfies a legitimate interest (which is not overridden by your data protection interests) such as the provision of updates on our services.

Data Retention

We will only hold your personal data for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes of its collection, including for the purposes of complying with applicable legal or reporting requirements.

Some categories of personal data we hold will need to be retained for longer periods, to determine the appropriate retention period for personal data, we consider the amount, nature and sensitivity of the personal data.

The potential risk of harm from unauthorised use or disclosure of personal data, the purpose for which they are processed and the applicable legal requirements and our contractual obligations.

In some circumstances we may anonymise personal data so that it can no longer be associated to you, in which case we may use such information without further notice to you.

Security

At Tidyquant we take great care in implementing, enforcing and maintaining security policies to help ensure the security of our services, sites and our user’s Personal Data. We have implemented generally accepted standards of technology and operational security to protect personal data from loss, misuse, alteration, or destruction. Only authorized Tidyquant personnel and third-party service providers are provided access to personal data, and these employees and service providers are required by NDA forms to treat this information as confidential.

Notification of breaches

If a breach of information was to occur, we would notify the user without delay, if any personal data held by Tidyquant on the user or on behalf of the user is lost, stolen, or there has been any unauthorised access to the personal data we would also contact authorities to ensure the highest chance of achieving a solution as quickly as possible as an issue such as this could result in a high risk to the user’s rights or freedoms.

Furthermore, Tidyquant cooperates with the user in investigating and remedying any such security breach. In any security breach involving personal data, Tidyquant shall immediately take remedial measures, including without limitation, reasonable measures to restore the security of the personal data and limit unauthorised or illegal dissemination of the personal data or any part thereof. Tidyquant maintains documentation regarding compliance with the requirements of the law.

Your rights

  • You may request access to the information we have about you at any time and may request us to rectify, update or restrict processing of such information. We may ask you to verify your identity and for more information about your request to ensure that no fraudulent party can gain access to information in this way.
  • We will use reasonable endeavours to ensure that your personal data is maintained and up to date. However, it is your duty to inform us of all changes to your personal data if you want to ensure that it is up to date, at your request we will update or delete your personal data accordingly.
  • You may, in certain circumstances have the right to request that we erase your personal data. We will respond to your request within the timescale required by applicable data protection laws and can and will only disagree with you if certain limited conditions apply. If your request is valid and accepted, we will delete your data but will generally assume that you would prefer us to keep a note of your name on our register of individuals who would prefer not to be contacted. That way, we will minimise the chances of you being contacted in the future where your data are collected in unconnected circumstances. If you would prefer us not to do this, you are free to say so.
  • We process your data based on consent you have provided to us, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time and have such data deleted and we will uphold that right at all times.

End users

Many of the services we provide are primarily used by large organisations. We provide services under a company or organisation contract, any personal data held is controlled by the company or organisation. When this is the case, your personal data will be subject to the privacy policy of your organisation, and questions about your information should be directed to your organisation.

Organisation account holders can:

  • Enter, modify or delete your registration information on your behalf
  • Restrict, suspend or terminate your access to our services
  • Access and retain your registration information and customer material
  • Control the interaction of third parties with your customer material

Changes to our policy

Any changes to this privacy policy will be posted on our website to ensure all parties are constantly able and aware of the information we collect, how we use it, and under what circumstances, if any, we disclose it. Where appropriate, we may notify you by email of any changes to our policy. Please check back frequently to see any updates or changes to our privacy notice.

Consent

By using the sites, you consent to the collection, use, and storage of your personal data by us in the manner described above and elsewhere on the sites. We reserve the right to make changes to this policy if we see fit. We will alert you to any such changes by updating this privacy policy on our website and possibly by email for large amendments. If we make material changes to this privacy policy that increase our rights to use personal data that we have previously collected about you, we will also obtain your consent either through an email to your registered email address or by prominently posting information about the changes on our sites.

We provide end to end solutions using ingestion and integration technologies to unearth your big data locked away in silos. Our goal is to take the data that you have stored away and unlock the keys to your success.
